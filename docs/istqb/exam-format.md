# The CTFL v4.0 exam

Everything worth knowing about the exam **before** you start studying: what is assessed, how it's scored and where the questions come from. Knowing the format is half the preparation — the exam is very predictable once you know how to read it.

## Format

| Fact | Value |
|---|---|
| Questions | 40, multiple choice |
| Scoring | 1 point per question, no penalty for wrong answers |
| Pass mark | 26 out of 40 (65 %) |
| Duration | 60 minutes (+25 % if the exam isn't in your native language: 75 min) |
| Answers | Single correct answer, unless the question states otherwise |

You can take it **online with remote proctoring or in person**, through an accredited exam provider (I took mine with Brightest). The certificate never expires.

## K-levels: how questions are asked

Every learning objective in the syllabus has a cognitive level, and that level determines the question type:

| Level | What it demands | Rough time per question |
|---|---|---|
| **K1** — Remember | Exact terminology and definitions | ~1 min |
| **K2** — Understand | Explain, compare, classify concepts | 1–2 min |
| **K3** — Apply | Solve a case: apply a technique to an example | ~3 min |

The exam includes **8 K3 questions**, and they decide who passes: almost all of them come from chapter 4 (test design techniques) and require practicing exercises, not just reading.

## The 6 syllabus chapters

The v4.0 syllabus has **6 chapters, 14 business outcomes and 64 learning objectives**. They don't carry equal weight in the exam:

| Chapter | Topic | Exam weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fundamentals of testing | High |
| 2 | Testing throughout the SDLC | Medium |
| 3 | Static testing | Low |
| 4 | Test analysis and design | **The highest** |
| 5 | Managing the test activities | High |
| 6 | Test tools | The lowest |

::: tip Key idea
Chapters 4 and 5 concentrate most of the questions, and chapter 4 also concentrates the K3s. If you're short on time, prioritize mastering the chapter 4 techniques: equivalence partitioning, boundary values, decision tables and state transitions.
:::

## Official documents (free download)

All the primary study material is free on the ISTQB website:

- **[CTFL v4.0 Syllabus](https://istqb.org/certifications/certified-tester-foundation-level-ctfl-v4-0/)** — the complete curriculum. It is THE source: every exam question derives from it.
- **Sample exams A, B, C and D** — on the same page, each with justified answers. The best way to calibrate your level.
- **[Official ISTQB Glossary](https://glossary.istqb.org/)** — the exact terminology K1 questions use. When in doubt between two definitions, this is the source of truth.
- Your local member board often provides translated syllabi and exams (in Spain, the [SSTQB](https://www.sstqb.es/)).
